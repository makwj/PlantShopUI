String of Turtles

Water Requirements

Only water when soil is completely dry

Light requirements

Bright indirect light to direct light

Useful Tips

For the best growth, place your String of Turtles immediately in front of a nice, bright window that gets mostly indirect light. Providing your plant with 2-3 hours of direct sun is very beneficial, but avoid direct sunlight for whole day.

Stephania Erecta

Water Requirements

Only water when soil is completely dry

Light requirements

Prefer bright indirect sunlight

Useful Tips

This plant can be placed in a sunny location when it is well-rooted and with good amount of foliage, but never under the scorching summer heat. Most caudices can get sunburned, which is irreversible. One beauty of Stephania Erecta is that it can be grown indoors, preferably near a window or a good source of light.

Monstera Deliciosa

Water Requirements

Only water when soil is completely dry

Light requirements

Prefer bright indirect sunlight

Useful Tips

Find a balance between sun and shade. If Monstera is given too much sun, the leaves will become yellow. If it's left in the dark, the plant will exhibit something known as negative phototropism, where new leaves grow towards the dark, rather than the light.

Monstera Peru

Water Requirements

Only water when soil is completely dry

Light requirements

Prefer bright indirect sunlight

Useful Tips

Tropical and jungle environments are pretty humid (no surprise there), and Monstera plants really love a bit of extra humidity. The easiest way to do this is to create a "pebble tray". Get your hands on a plastic or glass tray, fill it with river pebbles all the way up to the top, and then add water into the mix - coming near the top but not completely submerging the pebbles themselves. Leave the pebble tray near your plants and the evaporating water will add a bit of extra humidity to the air aorund your Monstera.
